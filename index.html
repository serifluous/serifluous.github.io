Schelling’s Segregation Model

Thomas Schelling’s Segregation Model shows that very small preferences for one’s neighbors to be of a certain type (e.g., race) can lead to widespread, or even total, segregation. To demonstrate, Schelling placed pennies and nickels on a grid and moved them around if they were “unsatisfied” by their neighbors. I’ve simulated his model using R. Read more on GitHub.
