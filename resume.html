Research Projects

  • “Spatial & Temporal Arbitrage: The Case of Marijuana in Continental America” Analyzed crowdsourced data from Price of Weed using R, expanding upon Zachary M. Jones’s project. Found that marijuana prices increase from east to west and have decreased over time as laws, enforcement, and social attitudes have grown lax. Seasonal trends vary among qualities, where high-quality marijuana shows higher prices and low-quality marijuana shows lower prices in the later half of the year following the seasonal harvest.
  • “Big Where It Counts: A Report on Estonia’s Recovery from the Recession” Analyzed Bank of Estonia data using MATLAB. Found that strict austerity measures and a shifting trade structure with growth in IT led to Estonia’s rapid recovery from the 2008 recession.
